Geography

The inseparable 44 Arsenal and 44 Elementary School

44 South Village is located in the west of the XinYi district. In Qing's dynasty, the 44 South Village is around a place named "Da Jia Rui Bao San Zhang Li". Due to geologicaly flat surface and the "Liu Gong" waterway, this place was either farmland or undeveloped.

During the Japanese era, this place was renamed to "SongShangZhuang". ZhaoHe years 13, 1938, SongShangZhuang merged with Taipei city. The land was still mostly farmland and pond. In addition, nearby mountain named "ShiShou" started coal mining development. There were not many activites until DaZheng years 13, 1924, a shotting range was built. ZhaoHe years 10, 1935, due to military needs, the Japanese government built warehouses for army and expanded QiLong road to 40 meters wide.

The Chinese government moved militants to SongShang district, where the current 44 South Village is, when they retreat from China. In 1948, 44 arsenal located in QingDao moved to the warehouse built by the Japanese. To settle the militants, 44 South, East, West Villages was built at the shooting range Japanese built.
