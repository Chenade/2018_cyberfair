Architecture

Woods and bamboos made the earliest building

In 1950's, beside the buildings that were left from Japanese colonial period, most of the bungalows were only construct in simple way. Built on government property, the area for each was very narrow. The material were also very simple, such as building the rooftop by straw or the wall by bamboo with clay. Lacking of the space forced people to build the add-on, second-floored add-on and the skylight became the common scenes in among the Village. With bad living condition and low financial ability, residents in 44 South Village were hard to improve the surroundings

Basic structure

The building in 44 South Village was built by woods, fences, lime, and tiles at first, then bricks were added to stabilize the structure afterwards.
